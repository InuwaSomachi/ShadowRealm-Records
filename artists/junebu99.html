Vates Vulgus

On moonless nights when the city's breath hangs heavy, the alleyways birth their chronicler. Junebu99, street alchemist and prophet of pavement, transmutes gutter whispers into golden verse. His tongue, sharpened on subway rails, caroes sagas in the forgotten tongue of fire escapes and bodega neon. The air hums with his liturgy - cracked 808 drums as sacramental bells, sampled sirens as celestial choirs. His scrolls (etched on pizza box parchment) tell of kings crowned with bottle caps and queens who dance on razor wire. Devotees leave offerings of spray cans and loosies at shrines beneath overpasses, praying for the blessing of his shadow.
